# Content Sourcing Policy

The project can scale only if each story has a clean rights trail.

## Allowed

- Public-domain classical texts, such as pre-modern Chinese zhiguai and chuanqi collections.
- Project Gutenberg texts that are public domain in the United States, after checking the book page and license notes.
- Wikisource pages where the source text and site license allow reuse, with attribution and source links.
- Creative Commons stories, if the exact license is compatible with reuse and attribution is preserved.
- Original editor-written commentary, summaries, translations, and annotations based on public-domain originals.

## Not Allowed

- Copying modern web fiction, forum posts,公众号 articles, social media posts, newsletter stories, or paid content without permission.
- "Pseudo-original" rewrites of copyrighted modern stories.
- Scraping large volumes from websites that do not grant reuse rights.
- Publishing unattributed text or AI-generated filler.

## Per-Story Required Fields

- `sourceBook`
- `originalAuthor`
- `sourceUrl`
- `sourceRights`
- `editorialMode`
- `editor`
- `lastReviewed`

## Editorial Standard

Each page should include a compact retelling, context, reading notes, and a visible link to the source. If a page is thin, it should remain unpublished until it has enough original editorial value.
